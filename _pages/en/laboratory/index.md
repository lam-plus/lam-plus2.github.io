<section class="page-shell lab-page">
	<header class="lab-hero">
		<div class="lab-hero-text">
			<h1>Laboratory</h1>
			<p class="lead">LAM+ — Laboratory for Multispectral Analysis and Artificial Intelligence for Sediments — is a multi-user scientific platform dedicated to the integrated analysis of sedimentary sequences at high resolution, combining non-destructive techniques and spectral, geochemical and physical data with artificial intelligence tools.</p>
			<p>The laboratory operates as a shared infrastructure focused on innovation in geosciences, human resource training and interinstitutional collaboration, producing reproducible and integrated data for sedimentological, palaeoenvironmental, climatic and environmental interpretations.</p>
		</div>
	</header>

	<section class="lab-section lab-highlights">
		<div class="lab-highlight-card">
			<h3>High resolution</h3>
			<p>Continuous data at millimetre, sub-millimetre or microscopic scale, depending on the technique applied.</p>
		</div>

		<div class="lab-highlight-card">
			<h3>Non-destructive analyses</h3>
			<p>Methods that preserve cores and samples are prioritised, maintaining their scientific and curatorial value.</p>
		</div>

		<div class="lab-highlight-card">
			<h3>Multiproxy integration</h3>
			<p>Combination of spectral, geochemical, physical, microscopic, visual and sedimentological data.</p>
		</div>

		<div class="lab-highlight-card">
			<h3>Artificial intelligence</h3>
			<p>Use of machine learning and data science for pattern recognition, data integration and interpretation.</p>
		</div>
	</section>

	<section class="lab-section">
		<h2>Analytical infrastructure</h2>
		<p>The LAM+ infrastructure combines hyperspectral imaging equipment, geochemical scanning, electron microscopy, physical properties profiling, visual documentation of samples and computational tools for the integration and interpretation of sedimentary data.</p>

		<div class="equipment-feature">
			<div class="equipment-text">
				<h3>VNIR/SWIR Hyperspectral Imaging System</h3>
				<p>The LAM+ hyperspectral imaging system acquires spectral cubes of sedimentary surfaces, in which each pixel contains a spectral signature. The infrastructure integrates cameras in the visible, near-infrared and short-wave infrared ranges, enabling investigation of compositional, mineralogical, textural and structural variations in sedimentary sections and solid samples.</p>
				<p>The system is particularly relevant for non-destructive analyses, spectral mapping, identification of subtle patterns and development of artificial intelligence models applied to sedimentary sequences.</p>
				<p class="equipment-status">Status: under installation / commissioning.</p>
			</div>

			<div class="equipment-image">
				<img src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/equipamentos/equip_HSI.png" alt="LAM+ VNIR/SWIR hyperspectral imaging system" />
			</div>
		</div>

		<div class="equipment-feature equipment-feature-reverse">
			<div class="equipment-text">
				<h3>Avaatech XRF Core Scanner</h3>
				<p>The XRF core scanner is dedicated to continuous acquisition of geochemical data from sedimentary cores by X-ray fluorescence. The technique measures elemental variations along sections at high spatial resolution without sample destruction.</p>
				<p>The data produced are fundamental for interpretations of terrigenous input, biogenic productivity, redox conditions, grain-size variations, abrupt environmental changes and sedimentary events. The equipment is also central to intercalibration protocols, analytical standardisation and integration with artificial intelligence methods.</p>
				<p class="equipment-status">Status: under installation / operational deployment.</p>
			</div>

			<div class="equipment-image">
				<img src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/equipamentos/equip_XRF.png" alt="LAM+ Avaatech XRF Core Scanner" />
			</div>
		</div>

		<div class="equipment-feature">
			<div class="equipment-text">
				<h3>Technical imaging and visual documentation</h3>
				<p>LAM+ will also organise visual documentation routines for samples, including technical photography, surface registration, metadata control and association of images with other analytical data produced in the laboratory.</p>
				<p>This step is essential to ensure traceability, curation and integration between the visual description of samples, spectral and geochemical data, and products derived from computational processing.</p>
				<p class="equipment-status">Status: under development.</p>
			</div>

			<div class="equipment-image">
				<img src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/equipamentos/equip_Photo.png" alt="Visual documentation and technical photography of sedimentary samples at LAM+" />
			</div>
		</div>

		<div class="equipment-feature equipment-feature-reverse">
			<div class="equipment-text">
				<h3>Scanning Electron Microscopy with EDS</h3>
				<p>Scanning electron microscopy with energy-dispersive spectroscopy enables characterisation of microstructures, grain morphology, mineral phases, precipitates, biogenic features and elemental composition in selected samples.</p>
				<p>SEM-EDS acts as a high-resolution complementary tool for investigating patterns observed with continuous techniques, deepening mineralogical, sedimentological, environmental and compositional interpretations.</p>
				<p class="equipment-status">Status: associated infrastructure / complementary analysis.</p>
			</div>

			<div class="equipment-image">
				<img src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/equipamentos/equip_MEV.png" alt="Scanning electron microscopy with EDS associated with LAM+" />
			</div>
		</div>

		<div class="equipment-feature">
			<div class="equipment-text">
				<h3>Physical properties profiling / MSCL</h3>
				<p>Physical properties profiling provides continuous measurements from sedimentary cores, including gamma density, P-wave velocity, electrical resistivity and magnetic susceptibility.</p>
				<p>These data provide a preliminary physical diagnosis of sections and help interpret lithological variations, compaction changes, sedimentary properties, compositional differences and patterns associated with provenance or depositional dynamics.</p>
				<p class="equipment-status">Status: infrastructure available at the host institution.</p>
			</div>

			<div class="equipment-image">
				<img src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/equipamentos/equip_MSCL.png" alt="Physical properties profiling system for sedimentary cores" />
			</div>
		</div>

		<div class="equipment-feature equipment-feature-reverse">
			<div class="equipment-text">
				<h3>Complementary analyses and support equipment</h3>
				<p>In addition to the main systems, LAM+ coordinates complementary analyses and support infrastructure for data preparation, validation and interpretation. This includes grain-size analysis, total organic carbon, carbonates, X-ray diffraction, colour imaging, portable XRF, technical photography, and sample storage and curation.</p>
				<p>These analyses are essential for calibrating, validating and interpreting products generated by continuous techniques, and for feeding computational models applied to sediment characterisation.</p>
				<p class="equipment-status">Status: complementary and collaborative infrastructure.</p>
			</div>

			<div class="equipment-image">

			</div>
		</div>
