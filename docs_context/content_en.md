## Short name

LAM+

## Full name

Laboratory for Multispectral Analysis and Artificial Intelligence for Sediments

## Short tagline

High-resolution innovation for the investigation of sedimentary sequences.

## Short description

LAM+ is a multi-user platform dedicated to the high-resolution analysis of sedimentary sequences, integrating optical, spectral, geochemical, physical and artificial intelligence tools.

## Institutional description

The Laboratory for Multispectral Analysis and Artificial Intelligence for Sediments — LAM+ — was designed to expand the capacity to investigate sedimentary sequences through rapid, reproducible, non-destructive and high-resolution methods. The laboratory integrates hyperspectral imaging, XRF core scanning, color analysis, sedimentary radiography, physical properties, electron microscopy and artificial intelligence tools to generate analytical products for sedimentological, paleoclimatic, geochemical and environmental interpretation.

## Mission

To integrate scanning, imaging, compositional analysis and artificial intelligence technologies to produce high-resolution data from sedimentary sequences, fostering methodological innovation, human resource training and scientific collaboration.

## Vision

To consolidate LAM+ as a reference infrastructure in multispectral analysis and artificial intelligence applied to sediments, contributing to advances in geosciences, paleoclimatology and the investigation of environmental change.

Sample Submission

### Initial text

Sample submission to LAM+ will be organized through an institutional workflow involving registration, technical screening, analytical planning, processing and data delivery. The goal is to ensure traceability, operational safety, analytical quality and efficient use of the multi-user infrastructure.

### Suggested workflow

1. Initial contact or form submission.
2. Project and principal investigator registration.
3. Sample description.
4. Technical screening.
5. Definition of applicable techniques.
6. Scheduling.
7. Sample reception and cataloging.
8. Analytical processing.
9. Quality control.
10. Delivery of data products.
